## A. Claims that are currently acceptable for controlled use
These are the claims that appear supportable enough for controlled scene-level use, subject to final master-table review:

1. Ping Yao is a historically important and exceptionally well-preserved traditional Chinese city.
2. Ping Yao can serve as a serious candidate anchor direction for explaining conservation boundary at an urban-heritage scale.
3. It is reasonable to use Ping Yao as one reference point for explaining that conservation involves authenticity, integrity, historical layers, management and balance, rather than simply returning a site to a brand-new state.
4. It is reasonable to connect the candidate to discussion of conservation, tourism pressure and liveability balance at a scene-level explanatory scale.

### Current handling recommendation
- treat these as `E3_supported` statements for scene-level conservation-boundary anchoring
- keep them in explanatory language, not proof language
- keep them inside `B_composite_teaching_scene` with explicit disclosure and boundary emphasis

## B. Claims that may be usable but must stay narrow
These are claims that may be usable, but only with tighter wording and no broad extrapolation:

1. Ping Yao preserves authentic layout, architectural style, building materials, construction craftsmanship and traditional technology.
2. Ping Yao is under authentic and integrated conservation through plans, intervention measures and management.
3. Its conservation programme can support limited discussion of historical layers, restoration guidelines, subsidies and integrated management.

### Current handling recommendation
- do not broaden these into totalizing claims about all Chinese conservation practice
- do not treat them as sufficient support for every technical intervention method or every building component claim
- keep them tied to one clearly bounded explanatory purpose: conservation boundary, not conservation triumph

## C. Claims that should NOT be used yet
These are claims that should remain out of downstream scene use until stronger or more specific support is added:

1. Any claim that the scene proves a complete correct model for all heritage conservation in China.
2. Any claim that Ping Yao alone proves a universal conservation doctrine.
3. Any precise technical conservation-method claim that goes beyond the current public-source bundle.
4. Any implication that a visually renewed or clean-looking image is evidence of correct conservation.
5. Any claim that turns the scene into tourism promotion, urban revitalisation success branding, or restoration miracle storytelling.
